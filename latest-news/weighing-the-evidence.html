Weighing the Evidence

12/3/2015

0 Comments

 
So, I'm trying to figure out whether or not I can yet claim to be "an Alaskan."  You be the judge and feel free to check back, as I keep finding more things to both columns!
EVIDENCE FOR:
  • Fairbanks homeowner
  • possess AK driver's license
  • electrify my car with a 50-ft. cord that runs from the hood of my car to the house
  • am eligible for next year's Permanent Fund Dividend check
  • own moose hide mukluks
  • left home Wednesday without a coat because it seemed warm (it was 12-F!)
  • told someone earlier that my husband was "Outside" for the month
  • have a freezer full of caribou meat
  • am taking very few showers
  • experienced one earthquake, slept through a couple others
  • just joined the Fairbanks Curling Club; this was strictly for business purposes, though I hear those curlers know how to PAR-TAY​
EVIDENCE AGAINST:​
  • have never caught a salmon with my "dip net" nor "run" a trapline
  • do not own a snow machine
  • have an indoor bathroom IN ADDITION to the outhouse
  • still marvel at the crazy lack of daylight this time of year
  • do not keep a blanket in my car to drape over the hood while at movies and such
  • don't mind if people keep their shoes on in my house
  • activate the washer/wiper fluid in the car this time of year, thinking this is still a good idea in arctic temperatures
My friend Abby tells me that to be a real Alaskan sourdough, one must pee in the Yukon River.  If that is, indeed, the litmus test, than I am definitely not yet an Alaskan.  I'll keep ya posted!
